Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 27, 2026. This Privacy Policy describes how Dashboard (“we,” “us,” or “our”) handles information when you visit this website, read this policy without signing in, or use the Dashboard after entering your personal PIN. The Dashboard is an internal sales operations and onboarding tool for authorized representatives only. It is not a public consumer website, not an app store product, and not intended for children or casual visitors.

1. Introduction and scope

This policy applies to all pages and features hosted under the Dashboard site, including the PIN lock screen, onboarding checklist, call scripts, text and email templates, sales tracker, Lead Finder, Lead Builder, payout setup, earnings pages, resources, owner contact information, and any other tools your manager enables for the team. By using the Dashboard you acknowledge that you have read this policy. If you do not agree, do not use the site.

We designed the Dashboard to support legitimate outbound sales work for our website agency. We do not sell advertising on the Dashboard, we do not run third-party analytics or tracking pixels on these pages, and we do not use the Dashboard to build marketing profiles of visitors who are only reading this policy or the Terms of Service without signing in.

2. Who operates the Dashboard

The Dashboard is operated by the business owner and manager identified in your team materials. The Dashboard is provided to sales reps on an invitation-only basis. Access is controlled by a PIN assigned to you personally. The Dashboard is not offered to the general public, and having the URL does not grant you any right to access features behind the PIN.

3. Information when you are not signed in

If you open only the Privacy Policy or Terms of Service from the lock screen, or if you open those pages in a browser where you have not successfully entered a valid PIN in the current session, we do not ask you to create an account and we do not require cookies for identity on those legal pages alone.

Your browser will still transmit standard technical data to whatever server hosts the site (for example IP address, browser type, and requested page). That data may appear in ordinary web server or hosting logs maintained by our hosting provider. We do not use that browsing of legal pages alone to identify you as an individual rep, assign you a profile, or sync data to our team database.

Reading this policy does not unlock the Dashboard. You cannot access Lead Finder, scripts, templates, or other protected areas without a valid PIN in that browser session.

4. Information we collect when you sign in

When you enter your PIN and unlock the Dashboard, we associate your session with a rep identity. The PIN itself is checked against a rep list maintained on the server (users.txt). We do not store your PIN in the cloud database as part of rep settings sync. What we do store and may sync, depending on features enabled by your manager, includes the following categories.

Rep name: Your display name as configured for your rep account. It may appear on the dashboard header, in the sales tracker, and when you mark a lead as called in Lead Finder so teammates can see who contacted a business.

Rep ID: An internal slug (for example a short identifier tied to your account) used to separate your saved settings from other reps. This is not your PIN.

Call script edits: Any changes you make to provided call scripts, including custom scripts you add, reorder, or delete in the script editor.

Text and email templates: Your customized outreach wording, subject lines, and any custom templates you create in the template builder.

Onboarding checklist progress: Which checklist steps you marked complete, stored so you can resume onboarding across sessions and devices when cloud sync is on.

Sales tracker entries: Deals you log, including amounts, business names, dates, and related fields you enter in the tracker.

Lead Builder preferences: Saved business name, phone number, Google Maps link, price tier selections, and related builder fields used to prepare outreach.

Payout method: The payment app you select (Cash App, Venmo, PayPal, or Zelle) and the payout link or Zelle contact you submit during payout setup. This is used so the owner can pay commissions. It is sensitive financial contact information and should be accurate.

Lead Finder called status: When you mark a lead as called, we record that status and may associate it with your rep name in a shared team database so duplicate calls are reduced.

Session unlock flag: A value in session storage indicating that you unlocked the site in this browser tab session. Closing the browser typically ends that session and requires PIN entry again.

Failed PIN attempt counter: If you enter wrong PINs, a counter and optional lockout timestamp may be stored in local storage on your device only to enforce a temporary lockout after repeated failures. This lockout data is not uploaded to our cloud database as part of standard rep sync.

5. Lead Finder and business lead data

Lead Finder displays business listings from our lead database. Those listings are compiled for outbound sales outreach and may include business names, phone numbers, street addresses, cities, states, categories, hours, Google ratings, review counts, and links to Google Maps. This information relates to businesses, not to private consumer households. We expect reps to use this data professionally and only for purposes aligned with team sales procedures.

When team sync is enabled, called status and the rep name tied to a call may be visible to other authorized reps and the manager in the shared database. Do not use Lead Finder to harass businesses, misrepresent your identity, or contact numbers for purposes unrelated to the team’s sales process.

6. How we use your information

We use the information described above to operate the Dashboard, personalize your experience, remember your edits, coordinate team lead status, support onboarding, display earnings-related information you enter, facilitate commission payouts through information you provide, and maintain security of the PIN gate. We do not use your rep data for unrelated advertising, and we do not sell rep profiles to third parties.

Your manager and the site owner may access aggregated or per-rep operational data as needed to run the team, review payout methods, audit lead activity, reset accounts, or troubleshoot sync issues.

7. Where data is stored

Some data is stored only in your browser (local storage) when cloud sync is disabled or unavailable. When your manager enables team sync, rep settings, payout records, lead called status, and the lead database may be stored in Supabase (a hosted PostgreSQL service). Supabase processes data on our behalf as a infrastructure provider. Their security and privacy practices apply to data held in their systems.

Static files such as the rep PIN list may be served from the same web host as the Dashboard. Lead data may also exist in CSV or JSON files used to populate the database during imports performed by the owner.

8. How long we keep information

We retain rep settings, tracker entries, templates, and payout information for as long as your access remains active and as needed for business operations, unless your manager or the owner deletes or resets your data. Lead records and called status may remain in the team database until removed during lead list updates. Server logs, if any, are retained according to hosting provider defaults. You should not rely on the Dashboard as your only record of commissions or closed deals; keep your own copies of important agreements with the owner.

9. Sharing and disclosure

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information in the following situations: with your manager and other authorized reps as part of normal team operations (for example called leads visible to the team); with service providers who host the site or database (such as Supabase or your web host); when required by law, subpoena, or legal process; or to protect the rights, safety, and security of the team, the owner, or others. We do not routinely disclose rep payout links publicly; they are intended for owner use in paying commissions.

10. Security measures

Access to the Dashboard requires a PIN. After five failed PIN attempts on a given browser, sign-in is blocked for approximately thirty minutes on that device. You are responsible for keeping your PIN confidential, not sharing it with other people, and not leaving an unlocked session unattended on a shared computer. No security measure is perfect; use reasonable care when accessing the Dashboard on personal or work devices.

Cloud database access uses industry-standard API keys configured for the site. The owner is responsible for securing project credentials and database row-level policies. Reps should report suspected unauthorized access to the owner promptly.

11. Third-party links and services

The Dashboard may link to external services including Google Maps, Telegram team chats, Website Agency groups, video hosts, email clients, and payment apps. If you leave the Dashboard and open those services, their own terms and privacy policies apply. We do not control third-party sites and are not responsible for their practices. Opening a maps link, sending a Telegram message, or using Venmo or Cash App happens outside this policy’s direct scope once you leave our pages.

12. Your responsibilities

You agree to provide accurate payout information, use lead data only for authorized sales activity, and notify the owner if you believe your PIN has been compromised. You should log out practically by closing the browser session when finished on a shared device. Do not attempt to bypass the PIN lock, scrape the site, or export lead data in bulk outside team rules.

13. Children’s privacy

The Dashboard is not directed to anyone under eighteen years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a minor has been given a PIN, contact the owner so access can be revoked.

14. International users

The Dashboard is operated for a United States–based sales team. If you access the site from another country, you understand that data may be processed in the United States or wherever our hosting providers operate. Laws in your location may differ from those described here.

15. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy at any time. The “Last updated” date at the top will change when we do. Continued use of the Dashboard after changes means you accept the revised policy. Material changes may also be communicated through team Telegram or manager announcements when appropriate.

16. Contact

Questions about this Privacy Policy or how your rep data is handled should be directed to the site owner after you sign in, or by email to fullprofessionalwebsites@outlook.com. For urgent security concerns (lost PIN, suspected breach), contact your manager or the owner immediately through your normal team channel.

End of Privacy Policy.

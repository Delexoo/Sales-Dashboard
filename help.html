1. What you are here to do

You are a cold caller on the Website Agency sales team. Your job is to contact local businesses that do not have a proper website, explain the free demo offer, quote the right price tier for their size, and hand qualified, interested leads to the owner and build team. You are not a web designer, developer, or account manager · you focus on the phone conversation and accurate lead handoff.

We sell professional websites to owner-run local businesses: plumbers, salons, restaurants, cleaners, contractors, and similar trades. Many still have no real site in 2026 and lose customers because of it. You find those businesses, call them, and move interested owners toward a paid website package.

You earn commission when a client pays upfront · not when you forward a lead or hear “maybe” on the phone. The owner builds the demo, handles texts, bookings, revisions, and payment collection after you pass a real lead. Your time on the phone matters, but your paycheck follows closed deals.

2. What we sell and how to quote

We offer four website tiers based on business size and complexity. You choose the tier on the call · the client does not pick from a menu themselves.

Sale prices (what the client pays upfront): $500, $700, $1,000, or $1,500 · these are the only four amounts you quote on the phone. Each tier also has a small monthly hosting fee (see below).

Your commission: 40% of the upfront sale price when the client actually pays · not 40% of the monthly fee, and not when you forward a lead. Example: a $700 sale pays you $280.

$500 tier · $500 upfront plus $5 per month hosting. Smallest or simplest businesses.

$700 tier · $700 upfront plus $20 per month.

$1,000 tier · $1,000 upfront plus $10 per month.

$1,500 tier · $1,500 upfront plus $10 per month. Larger or more complex businesses.

Remember: sale prices are always $500, $700, $1,000, or $1,500 upfront. Monthly hosting ($5–$20 depending on tier) covers hosting on our side and is not part of your 40% commission.

Two delivery styles exist: Direct Link (demo plus pay-by-text) and Booking (demo plus a meeting). Ask the owner which they prefer · do not choose for them.

Your pitch starts with a free demo website so they can see what they are missing before they pay. You do not send the demo link yourself; after a qualified conversation you collect details in Lead Builder and click Send lead on the website.

3. How you get paid

Commission rate: 40% of the upfront sale amount when the client actually pays. The upfront amount is always one of the four sale prices · $500, $700, $1,000, or $1,500.

Examples at 40%: $500 sale → $200 to you · $700 → $280 · $1,000 → $400 · $1,500 → $600.

There is no hourly wage. You are not paid for time dialing, for forwarding leads, or for “maybe” responses. Payment happens after the client pays upfront and the owner confirms the close. Set up your payout method in Settings so the owner can pay you through Cash App, Venmo, PayPal, or Zelle.

Team policy: every deal you personally close pays you the full 40% · no hidden splits on your own sale. Owner-closed deals may follow a separate team split policy described in course training.

4. Your first day · step by step

Step 1 · Sign in. Open the dashboard URL and sign in with the access your manager gave you. Do not share your login with anyone. On a shared computer, sign out and close the browser when you finish.

Step 2 · Complete course training. In the sidebar under Course, work through all six modules in order: Start Here, The Business, Setup Accounts, Preferences, Platform Tour, and Everyday Tasks. Watch the videos and use the chapter summaries. Skipping training causes confusion later.

Step 3 · Finish Get started. Open Get started (also linked from Setup Accounts in the course) and complete the setup survey · set up your payout method first, then optionally join the team Telegram chat. Leads are always sent through Lead Builder on the website.

Step 4 · Set preferences. In module 4 (Preferences) or Settings, choose light/dark theme, set your display name, and add a profile photo if you want teammates to recognize you.

Step 5 · Walk through Everyday Tasks. Open Everyday Tasks (module 6) and follow the six-step daily loop with the built-in buttons to Lead Finder, Call Scripts, and Lead Builder. Use this page as your rhythm reminder every workday.

Step 6 · Make your first practice pass. Open Lead Finder, pick one business, read the call script, and walk through Lead Builder without sending a live lead until your manager says you are ready.

Step 7 · Start calling. Follow the everyday workflow in section 5 below. Log closes on the Dashboard when deals pay out.

5. Every workday · the core loop

This is the repeating cycle successful reps follow (the same six steps shown in Everyday Tasks, module 6):

1. Open Lead Finder · Browse businesses that already do not have a website. The default filter is No website · use category chips and review filters if you want to narrow the list. The number beside Lead Finder in the sidebar shows how many active leads are available.

2. Pick a business and click Build Lead · Choose one card from the list and click Build Lead. This opens Lead Builder with business details prefilled and marks the lead as in progress for you.

3. Call and pitch · Dial from the card or use the phone field in Lead Builder. Keep Call Scripts open in another tab. Introduce yourself honestly, offer the free demo, quote the tier that fits, and answer basic questions. If they are not interested, mark Not interested in Lead Finder and move on politely · do not send the lead.

4. Fill Lead Builder when they are interested · Confirm business name, pick the price tier you quoted ($500, $700, $1,000, or $1,500), paste the Google Maps link, enter phone and owner name, and choose Direct Link or Booking based on what the owner wants. The Send lead button shows a completion percentage until every required field is filled.

5. Send the lead · Click Send lead when the form is complete. Your submission is saved on the website · no copy-paste needed. The business appears in Pending businesses on the Dashboard, and management is notified to build the demo and handle next steps.

6. Update status in Lead Finder · After a successful contact or handoff, mark the business Complete so the team knows it is handled. Use Pending in the ⋮ menu if you still need to call back. Quick Save (bookmark icon) is only for your personal short list · it does not change team status.

7. Check the Dashboard · Review your monthly goal, commission earned, and sales count. Open Pending businesses when you need to follow up on sent leads.

8. Log paid deals · When a client pays and your manager confirms the close, record it on the Dashboard under Log a sale · enter sale amount and business name, then click Save sale. Your progress ring and commission total update automatically.

9. Repeat · Move to the next lead. Rejection is normal; volume and improvement over time drive results.

7. Your account, sync, and security

Your rep account ties together training progress, scripts, tracker sales, Lead Finder status, and settings. When you are signed in, changes save to your account and can follow you across devices using the same login.

Keep your sign-in private. Do not leave an unlocked session on a shared computer. Sign out when you are done. Report lost or compromised access to your manager right away.

Do not export lead lists for non-team use or share business phone numbers outside approved workflows.

9. Dashboard · revenue and sales tracking

The Dashboard is your income command center for the month.

Goal · Your monthly commission target. Tap the amount to edit it · the default is $1,000, but set whatever motivates you. Changes save automatically.

Commission earned · Running total of commission from sales you have logged this month (40% of each logged upfront sale amount).

Sales · How many deals you have logged.

Progress ring · Shows percent of your goal reached based on commission earned vs. your goal.

Log a sale · Enter the upfront sale amount (for example $500, $700, $1,000, or $1,500) and an optional business name, then click Save sale. Commission is calculated for you.

Sales list · Every logged sale appears below the form. You can edit or delete an entry if you made a mistake.

Pending businesses · Expand this panel to see leads you sent through Lead Builder. See section 11 for actions on each row.

The tracker is for your motivation and record-keeping. Official commission still follows manager confirmation and client payment. Keep your payout method current in Settings.

10. Lead Finder · finding businesses to call

Lead Finder lists local businesses from the team database. Default view is Active · leads nobody on the team has removed or finished in a way that hides them from the pool.

List views (dropdown at top): Active, Quick Save (bookmarked leads), Completed (yours and team), Not interested, and Removed.

Website filter · No website (default), Website, or All. Most reps focus on No website because that is our target market.

Reviews filter · Filter by Google review count (1–5) or All. Useful when you want smaller businesses first.

Categories · When available, click category chips to prioritize certain business types at the top of the list.

Refresh · Pulls the latest leads and team status. Use if counts look stale.

Each lead card shows business name, category, phone, address, hours, maps link, reviews, and action buttons.

Quick Save (bookmark icon) · Saves a lead to your Quick Save list without changing team status.

Build Lead · Opens Lead Builder with details prefilled and marks the business as in progress for you. You stay on your current list view.

⋮ menu · Change workflow status: Pending, Complete, Not interested, Removed, or restore a removed lead. Only the rep who set Pending, Complete, or Not interested can clear that status for that business.

When team sync is on, status changes are visible to other reps so two people do not call the same business blindly. Only mark Complete when you made a genuine contact attempt or finished handoff per team rules.

11. Pending businesses panel

On the Dashboard, expand Pending businesses to see leads you sent through Lead Builder. This is your short list of businesses handed off and awaiting build or follow-up.

If the panel is empty, the message reads: send a lead from Lead Builder to add a business here. Build Lead alone does not populate this list · you must click Send lead when the form is complete.

Each row shows name, time added, category, and phone when available. Primary actions:

Call · Dial the number if listed.

Maps · Open the Google Maps link when available.

⋮ menu · Cancel removes pending status and returns the lead to the active pool for you. Not interested marks the business not interested team-wide.

The panel shows a pending count on the toggle and refreshes when you send new leads or change status.

12. Lead Builder · handing off an interested lead

Lead Builder is the form you fill when an owner wants to move forward. Open it from Build Lead on a Lead Finder card or directly from Daily tools.

Fields (all required before send):

Business Name · Usually prefilled from Lead Finder.

Price · Tap the tier you quoted: $500, $700, $1,000, or $1,500.

Google Maps · Paste the listing URL so the build team can verify the business.

Phone · Owner or business line. A Call button appears when the number is valid.

Preference · Direct Link (demo plus pay-by-text) or Booking (demo plus a meeting). Ask the owner which they want.

Owner Name · Decision-maker you spoke with.

The Send lead button shows a completion percentage until every field is filled. The form autosaves as you type. Use Clear to reset the form.

When you send, you see a confirmation screen · management is notified, we build the website, handle payment collection, and host the meeting with the owner when needed. You will be notified when your commission is ready.

Double-check tier, phone, and maps link before sending. Wrong details waste build time and hurt credibility.

Lead Builder also has an inline step-by-step help panel on the page if you need a visual walkthrough while filling the form.

17. Professional conduct on calls

Identify yourself truthfully and state why you are calling. Follow applicable telemarketing and communication laws in your area. Respect do-not-call requests immediately.

Do not harass, threaten, or mislead business owners. Do not use lead phone numbers for pranks or unrelated spam. Do not guarantee outcomes the owner has not authorized.

Verify critical details when something on the card looks wrong · old listings happen. Thank uninterested owners politely and move on; rejection is part of cold calling.

18. Troubleshooting

Cannot sign in · Confirm you are using the correct access from your manager. Try another browser or device. Contact your manager if you still cannot get in.

Lead Finder empty or error · Click Refresh. Hard refresh the browser (Ctrl+Shift+R on Windows, Cmd+Shift+R on Mac). Confirm you are signed in. If the problem persists, report it via Bug Bounty or Telegram with what you clicked and what you expected.

Status not syncing · Team features need an active connection. If sync is temporarily down, changes may be device-only until connection returns. Refresh the page after a minute.

Scripts not saving · Stay signed in and avoid private browsing modes that block storage. Open Settings to confirm your account is loaded.

Goal or sales not updating · Edit the goal field directly on the Dashboard · it saves on blur. For sales, use Log a sale with a valid upfront amount. If numbers look wrong after editing, refresh once.

Send lead button disabled · Fill every required field · watch the completion percentage on the button. Pick a price tier and delivery preference explicitly.

Wrong payout info · Update immediately in Settings; incorrect details delay commission.

Page looks broken · Try another browser, disable extensions, or file Bug Bounty with steps to reproduce.

Console messages about browser extensions · Lines mentioning redirect blockers or disabled features often come from extensions, not the dashboard. They are usually safe to ignore unless a page feature actually fails.
